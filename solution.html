Empower Your Shower

The Solution

It doesn't take any brain power to save water, just a little creative thinking!

To Save Water at Home:

1. Take shorter showers

2. Rinse your razors in the sink

3. It takes about 70 gallons of water to fill a bathtub, so showers are generally the more water-efficient way to bathe

4. A cup of coffee takes 55 gallons of water to make, with most of that H2O used to grow the coffee beans. So cut back on it (you really don't need the caffine)

To Save Water on Your Lawn:

The average pool takes 22,000 gallons of water to fill, and if you don't cover it, hundreds of gallons of water per month can be lost due to evaporation.

Use a broom, not a hose, to clean driveways and sidewalks
